  Reiki for Youngsters
 

"Come to the edge, he said.
They came.
He pushed them… and they flew.”
(Guillaume Apollinaire)

 

 

Reiki Ryoho can be easily learned and practiced by anyone, including children and teenagers. The benefits are as varied as those who receive them.

Here are the thoughts of some young practitioners:

Reiki awakens, cleanses, and re-energizes
“Reiki is something that requires belief, rather than understanding. The presence of the energy is simply felt, just as one might feel love towards the outdoors, or have an intuition about a situation, yet not be able to explain or to understand the experience. Similarly, all one needs to do, when getting in touch with Reiki, is to let go and to concentrate on one’s inner reality and the present moment. When giving Reiki, it is important to follow the first, most intuitive feelings, and not to worry about methods, results, or the next moment. Furthermore, Reiki allows one to connect to others on an “inter-personal” level through hands-on treatments which leave the receiver relaxed and content, and the giver satisfied at being able to aid someone else for a change. My encounters with the Reiki energy have been what I would call “personal awakenings”. As I exit the meditative state experienced during a Reiki session, I am usually overwhelmed by wonder and awe, full of curiosity and questions, while simultaneously feeling awake, re-energized, and “cleansed”.” (Zhenya, age 15)

Reiki disentagles, soothes, comforts, and brings joy
“Reiki is something which is present in everyone, but we are just too entangled in our daily chores to have time for anything else. Reiki is the healer present in everyone, if one would only take time for oneself, at least a few minutes, a few magical minutes here and there. The energy feels like a soothing layer of balm cooling the mind and swishing away pestering thoughts. Once you are in Reiki, you discover a world, oh so very beautiful and enchanting, a world which has always been there for you, just waiting to be discovered while you were dormant. Reiki is not a godly gift, given to a few chosen people; it is there for everyone. When sharing the energy with somebody else, you tend to feel the rhythm of the receiver, while the receiver feels sleepy and relaxed. Reiki, in other words, is comfort, pure comfort and joy.” (Paluck, age 17)

Reiki heals emotional conflicts and depression
In every person’s life, there comes a time when they find themselves surrounded by many problems and obstacles which lead them to depression and other emotional imbalances.. I personally have been through those problems since I am a teenager and a high school student and this is a time when most of us feel unsatisfied with what is going on in our lives. There were moments when I found myself completely withdrawn and isolated and no one seemed to understand me. That was the time when I really needed help and luckily I heard of Reiki. Reiki helped me get through the emotional conflicts and the depression that I experienced over a period of time. When I practiced Reiki for the first time I felt a very warm energy flowing along with my blood. I felt as if I was in warm water for a moment. I felt as if my body was lifted up in the air. I guess that was the moment when it started healing me. Reiki is also meant to heal physical disorders such as pain or headaches but it has helped me more emotionally than physically. (Mariam, age 16)

Reiki brings clarity and inner peace
When I went to receive my first Reiki attunement, I had no expectations and was not certain what benefits Reiki had for me. I went nonetheless, since it did not seem to hold any adverse consequences. In retrospect, I’m quite glad I began my training in the use of Reiki. Along with Reiki, I learned and began to meditate.The largest benefit I have experienced is a sense of relaxation – of tranquility. I have come to realize that many of the problems in my life are self-generated through my fears and worries. Now being able to confront those worries and fears, and contemplate them, I can regain my life, so to speak. Through relaxation and realization of what my true obstacles are, being able to observe those false problems generated in my mind, I am happier than I was before.I consistently meditate and use Reiki because I can release my worries, at least while I am meditating or working with Reiki. With a clear mind, I can then look at my problems from a more third-person perspective, and analyse and prioritize them in a structured fashion. In this regard, I am more organized and more at peace with my life than I have ever been in the past. This, I know, is due to Reiki and meditation.Another large problem that Reiki and meditation have helped me with is my temper. Although many of my friends and relatives cannot attest to how I am when I am mad, my temper is actually quite short. With a clearer and peaceful mind, I can realize when my anger is growing. This way, I can question why I am getting mad and how to go about approaching those causes before I lose my temper. Therefore, I can go about resolving these issues calmly and I do accomplish more that way. (James, age 15)

Reiki restores harmony to mind, body, and situations
“I received my first Reiki attunement a year ago, and now, as I look back, I wouldn’t say that I have become a completely different person. However, one thing has changed: I am now able to restore harmony to people’s bodies, minds and situations, without speaking any words or taking any actions. Moreover, Reiki allows me to go beyond people’s emotional and mental defenses or to connect with them on a deeper level in order to assist them in improving their consciousness and overall balance. I am now a second-degree Reikier, able to send Reiki to people and situations from great distances. Reiki seems as magical to me now, as ever, since I still can’t logically understand how one is able to harness an energy of a different frequency and use it to create harmony. However, I have stopped worrying about that and simply been allowing myself to believe in the power of hope and good will. I have also come to realize that with personal desire and attachment come headaches and fatigue, so now I simply detach and allow the energy to flow.” (Sakshi, age 16)

Reiki heals, empowers, revitalizes, and transforms
Whenever someone asks me what Reiki is, I say in the simplest terms “a healing energy.” For me, Reiki both empowers the spirit and the mind because you are taking an active role in your own well-being. You use Reiki to revitalize yourself both mentally and physically. Reiki is also so beautiful because it goes beyond you and connects you to something much larger. Sounds very Star Trek but it’s real, deep seated, and wise. Reiki is not only a practice but its principles also spill over into other parts of your life, as well as your personal philosophy. It can transform your intentions and attitude towards the positive. Everyone can benefit from Reiki in their life. (Rosie, age 16)

Reiki brings purity and joy of living
Reiki… It’s such a small word, but it has had such a big impact on my life. Reiki helps me get out of bed every morning and stay positive. It tells me that everything is going to be alright. It makes the harshness of my days go away. In fact, I don’t feel any negativity when I am in my "zone"...I feel pure as if I had just come out of the shower, as if I had flipped my body inside out and taken a dip in a pool of fresh water. To me this is what Reiki is. To you, it could be something else, something better, something to live for. I now want to live longer because I want to experience this magic longer; the longer, the better. (Mouska, age 16)
