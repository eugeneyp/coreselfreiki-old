  Komyo ReikiDo
 

“Go placidly
In the midst of praise or blame!”
(Komyo ReikiDo motto)

 

 

Buddhist monk Hyakuten Inamoto is the founder of the Kyoto Healing Centre where he holds weekly classes and Reiki shares for both Japanese and foreign students.

Although officially belonging to the Jodo-Shu or Pure Land branch of Japanese Buddhism, Inamoto Sensei is an independent monk who seeks authentic spiritual experiences. When asked about his temple or monastery, Inamoto Sensei answers in his typical wise yet humorous fashion: “I do not live in a temple or monastery. I am the temple. People are the temple. My temple is where I am. It is a mobile temple.”

Komyo ReikiDo, the system Inamoto Sensei has created, emphasizes both the therapeutic aspect and the spiritual aspect of Reiki Ryoho (the healing of the body and the mind, as well as the realization of the state known as Anshin Ritsumei, or enlightenment).

The motto of Komyo ReikiDo, “Go placidly in the midst of praise or blame” intends to remind practitioners that through Reiki Ryoho, we are accessing non-dual conscious vibration and that our ability to do so depends on our capacity for inner stillness. Furthermore, although lineage is important, in Komyo ReikiDo, deeds are the criteria of a good Reiki practitioner and teacher. What one does, speaks, and thinks, how one lives one’s life on a day to day basis, is considered to be of primary importance. This understanding is supported by Buddhist philosophy. According to Dhammapada, “Everything whatsoever has the mind as the source. The mind is the master builder and of the mind is everything made. If with an impure mind, one speaks or acts, suffering follows him in the same way as the wheel follows the ox that draws the cart. The mind is the master builder and of the mind is everything made. If with a pure mind, one speaks or acts, happiness follows him like his shadow that never leaves him.”

Inamoto Sensei learned Reiki from Mrs. Chiyoko Yamaguchi who had trained with Dr. Chujiro Hayashi, one of Usui Sensei’s original students.
